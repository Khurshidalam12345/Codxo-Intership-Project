Tribute to Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

"The Missile Man of India" and the People's President

Biography

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, born on October 15, 1931, was an Indian aerospace scientist who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. He was intimately involved in India's civilian space program and military missile development efforts, earning him the nickname 'Missile Man'. His dedication to education and his passion for innovation inspired generations, making him one of the most beloved public figures in India.

Notable Works

  • Development of India's first satellite launch vehicle (SLV-III)
  • Key role in India's Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998
  • Author of inspiring books like "Wings of Fire" and "Ignited Minds"
  • Advocate for youth empowerment and education through various initiatives

Famous Quotes

"Dream is not that which you see while sleeping, it is something that does not let you sleep."
"If you want to shine like a sun, first burn like a sun."
